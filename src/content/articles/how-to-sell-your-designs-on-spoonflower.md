<p><strong>Short answer:</strong> upload your artwork to Spoonflower, set it for sale in the Marketplace, and earn a commission every time someone orders it on fabric, wallpaper or home decor. You design once; Spoonflower handles the printing, shipping and payments. Here's how to do it — and do it well.</p>

<h2>How do you sell designs on Spoonflower?</h2>
<p>In five steps:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Create a seamless design</strong> — artwork that tiles perfectly, with no visible joins.</li>
<li><strong>Upload it</strong> to your Spoonflower account and choose a repeat type (basic, half-drop, half-brick, mirror or centre). Since you select this inside Fabric or Wallpaper types, you can select a different type for each.</li>
<li><strong>Proof it online</strong> to check it's seamless, and see how it looks applied to many products.</li>
<li><strong>List it for sale</strong> in the Marketplace, across fabric, wallpaper and home-decor products.</li>
<li><strong>Earn a commission</strong> on every sale — design once, then sell it again and again.</li>
</ol>

<h2>What can you sell on Spoonflower?</h2>
<p>Once uploaded, your design is available across Spoonflower's whole range — quilting and apparel fabrics, peel-and-stick and traditional wallpaper, plus home decor such as cushions, curtains, napkins and tea towels. One strong pattern can sell on dozens of different products without any extra work from you.</p>

<h2>Do you need special software or skills?</h2>
<p>You need design software you can already use — Adobe Illustrator or Photoshop (or Affinity, or Procreate - and there are others). The skills that actually matter are making a <em>seamless repeat</em> and attractive designs with good contrast. You don't need to be a Spoonflower expert to start; you need to be a designer who can prepare files correctly.</p>

<h2>How do Spoonflower royalties work?</h2>
<p>Spoonflower pays you a commission on every item sold that features your design. Uploading and listing are free, and because it's print-on-demand there's no stock to hold, nothing to ship and no admin on your side. (Check Spoonflower's current commission rates, as the tiers change from time to time.)</p>

<h2>How do you make patterns that actually sell?</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Nail the seamless repeat</strong> — you don't want complaints from customers after they ordered.</li>
<li><strong>Get colour accurate</strong> — you can choose to order swatches from the USA site, but shipping adds up.</li>
<li><strong>Design in collections</strong> — coordinating patterns sell together.</li>
<li><strong>Follow trends, but add your voice</strong> — research what's selling, then make it yours.</li>
<li><strong>Think beyond fabric</strong> — wallpaper and full-wall murals are higher-value products.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Spoonflower selling FAQ</h2>
<h3>Is it free to sell on Spoonflower?</h3>
<p>Yes — uploading and listing your designs is free. You earn a commission only when an item sells.</p>
<h3>How much can you earn selling on Spoonflower?</h3>
<p>It depends on how many designs you list and how well they match demand. It's passive income: each design can keep earning for years after you upload it.</p>
<h3>Do I have to handle printing or shipping?</h3>
<p>No. Spoonflower prints, packs and ships every order and handles the payment. You just design and list.</p>
<h3>What makes a design sell on Spoonflower?</h3>
<p>A clean seamless repeat, on-trend themes, and coordinating collections rather than one-off prints.</p>
