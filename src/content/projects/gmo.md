introduction: |-
  <h3 id="">I heard about the GMO Conspiracy</h3><p id="">Around the campfire one night, an acquaintance told me of her exasperation that big corporations like Bayer and Monsanto are trying to quietly 'disappear' GMO labelling from food in the EU.</p><p id="">‍</p><h3 id="">I suggested a whatsapp video</h3><p id="">I explained that a whatsapp animation that can be forwarded and shared, would help get the word out. </p><p id="">She has a zero budget for fighting these things, so I offered to do the animation for free.<br>‍<br>She was excited about the idea, and sent me lots of information, which I condensed into shorter copy to make sure the animation is kept short - it only needs to quickly impart the important info, and get people to click to read more, sign the petition and forward on to friends. </p><p id="">‍</p><h3 id="">I wanted it to be sharable and hopefully go viral</h3><p id="">When having a zero budget, you've got to be smart in how you communicate and get the message out, so I particularly wanted the animation to encourage people to share it. </p><p id="">Note, that whatsapp has a limit of 5MB for files, which is like an average quality image (but this is an animation, so many images and a larger file), so it's important to keep the wording short = less reading time = shorter animation length (in seconds) = less compression and quality loss in the final file.</p><p id="">Unfortunately, another person who didn't understand the need to keep copy short was invited to review my German copy, and inevitably, many words were added - I made it work though. </p><p id="">‍</p><h3 id="">Impact</h3><p id="">When I started the project on August 11th, the petition had just under 5k signatures. By 21st August (10 days later) it had 86,000 signatures - <strong id="">it's now accelerated to about 10,000 signatures per hour!</strong></p>
