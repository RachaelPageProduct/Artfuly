<p>*with my husband who took the month off work completely</p><p>‍</p><h3>Why we spent a whole month in one place</h3><p>Last winter we travelled around Colombia, spending around 3 days in each place, and we got tired of bus stations, backpacking, finding the new wifi password and a place to eat. So we decided to stay in once place this year, which also makes it much easier for me to get on with work, and we knew we'd have a stable wifi connection up front.</p><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" data-rt-type="image" data-rt-align="fullwidth"><div><img alt="" src="https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/5f6c7756eae6eb60a54c6c38/65bb9fa91bf2d1343f35916c_IMG_20240125_131636767%20copy.jpg" width="auto" height="auto" loading="lazy"></div></figure><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" data-rt-type="image" data-rt-align="fullwidth"><div><img alt="" src="https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/5f6c7756eae6eb60a54c6c38/65bba47cfc9f1d95f43ada88_Frame%202.jpg" width="auto" height="auto" loading="lazy"></div></figure><h3>Temperatures - Springtime in the middle of winter!</h3><p>During our stay the temperature varied from 12 degrees to 22 degrees Celcius during the day. In Germany near Berlin it ranged from minus 12 to around zero during the day - one day it was 30 degrees higher here in Cefalu than Berlin!</p><p>Usually we would be walking around in a t-shirt and light jacket - when sitting around outside sometimes a jumper as well is good to have.</p><p>‍</p><h3>Where we stayed</h3><p>I found an airbnb with an open fire in the lounge, a well equipped kitchen with washing machine, a nice terrace with a huge table, a front balcony overlooking the sea and a bathroom with a bath.</p><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" data-rt-type="image" data-rt-align="fullwidth"><div><img alt="" src="https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/5f6c7756eae6eb60a54c6c38/65bb9e704de62280d4abc893_IMG_20240116_150950509%20copy.jpg" width="auto" height="auto" loading="lazy"></div></figure><p>‍</p><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" data-rt-type="image" data-rt-align="fullwidth"><div><img alt="" src="https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/5f6c7756eae6eb60a54c6c38/65bb9a2da1358e3260442406_IMG_20240106_160340%20copy.jpg" width="auto" height="auto" loading="lazy"></div></figure><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" data-rt-type="image" data-rt-align="fullwidth"><div><img alt="" src="https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/5f6c7756eae6eb60a54c6c38/65bb9ee5410036e575189d7c_Frame%201.jpg" width="auto" height="auto" loading="lazy"></div></figure><p>‍</p><h3>My work and how I work</h3><p>When I'm not travelling I have a large desk with a 2-screen set-up - you can find out more in the video below</p><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-video w-richtext-align-fullwidth" style="padding-bottom:75%" data-rt-type="video" data-rt-align="fullwidth"><div><iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/mFViy5EkBTw?start=7" title="Perfect Equipment for UX and Product Design" scrolling="no" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="true"></iframe></div></figure><p>‍</p><p>When I travel, I take my macbook air, a wacom tablet and a small microphone for video creation.</p><p>As of Jan 2024, I have 1 main client (Product Design online work), 2 ongoing private students (I create educational content and deliver it to them in online meetings one-on-one), and 1 contractor helping me with youtube optimisation who I also meet online.</p><p>My work in order of income priority is:</p><ol start="1"><li><strong>Full-time freelance contract work</strong> (when I do 35-40 hrs per week on this I dial back other commitments)</li><li><strong>Part-time freelance work for clients</strong> (each client could be from 1-20 hours per week and it's changeable)</li><li><strong>Private tutoring &amp; mentoring</strong> online</li><li><strong>Career Foundry</strong> - I was a tutor and marked students assignments</li><li><strong>Creating my own courses</strong> in Product Design, UX Design &amp; Visual or UI Design</li><li><strong>Creating content</strong> to market my courses</li><li><strong>Doggy day care</strong> - this doesn't bring in much of an income, but it's a lifestyle choice to look after someone elses dog, walk it early in the morning, and hang out together all day in my summer garden (I only do this in the summer months)</li></ol><p>There's a lot of flexibility in the above, since when I don't have urgent client work I can fill gaps with no. 5, 6 and 7 to build educational content &amp; marketing content like videos and PDF slides to help students learn design.</p><p>‍</p><h3>How we got to Cefalu</h3><p>We flew direct from Berlin to Catania with Easyjet. In the winter months there seems to be only 1 direct flight per week. After landing at Catania airport we walked about 800m to the Fontarossa train station and were lucky to immediately jump on a train to Messina. At Messina we changed for a train to Palermo which stops at Cefalu. It was really easy and very scenic since most of the train line hugs the coast!. Be aware though that there aren't that many trains per day, so be armed with train information in advance and a few options.</p><p>‍</p><h3>Transport</h3><p>We didn't want the expense of hiring a car for a month, and didn't want to travel around the island in off season since not much is going on, most restaurants are closed etc, so we committed to walking the 50 minutes down the hill to the town and an hour back uphill with all our food and water several times per week! This was the kind of exercise that we'd never do unless forced to by way of needing to eat, and it was quite nice.</p><p>‍</p><h3>Water</h3><p>It's supposed to be possible to drink the water on Sicily, but it tastes kind of oily and I noticed that when running a bath it really puts you off wanting to drink any of it. We always bought bottled 'frizzante' water (with gas bubbles) and made tea and coffee with the tap water, although we eventually even used bottled water for hot drinks since the tap water put us off so much. On one of our walks we saw locals filling up large containers in their car from a tap high up in the hills, roughly in this location - 37.999842, 14.026073 - and that water tasted great.</p><p>‍</p><h3>Top Places we visited and Hiked to</h3><p>We did walking day trips to:</p><h4>A random Beach walk</h4><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth"
